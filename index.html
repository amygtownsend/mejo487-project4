Franklin Street Restaurants

Franklin Street is home to over 100 restaurants frequented often by UNC students and Chapel Hill and Carrboro dwellers alike. The popular subzone consists of restaurants alongside East Franklin St., West Franklin St., West Rosemary St., East Main St. and East Weaver St. and represents a wide range of cuisines.

Out of 113 restaurants in total, the oldest restaurants on Franklin Street are Carolina Coffee Shop and Sutton's Drug Store, which opened in 1922 and 1923 respectively. These restaurants are significantly older than any others on Franklin Street with a gap of 50 years between Sutton's Drug Store and the next oldest restaurants, Ye Olde Waffle Shop and McDonald's, both of which opened in 1973.

All the restaurants that opened between 1923 and 1973 have closed at this point, a reflection of the fast-moving nature of Franklin Street's restaurant scene. According to the Downtown Chapel Hill Partnership, the businesses on Franklin Street from January 2013 to January 2018 had a retention rate of 75.6 percent, meaning that one-fourth of the businesses closed just in these recent past five years.

Franklin Street Restaurant Openings Over Time

Franklin Street welcomed in 38 new restaurants in 2018, making up just over a third of all restaurants currently open. The high amount of newly opened restaurants distinguishes Franklin Street as a very popular location for business ventures. Some of the newest foods to join the scene include bubble tea and acai bowls, both of which are very popular food trends.

At least one restaurant serving American cuisine has opened each year since 2013, which is not surprising considering the cuisine's popularity. New coffee and tea shops and pizza places have opened every year since 2015 and 2016 respectively. The most recent trend in coffee and tea is easily enough linked to the bubble tea craze, but there is no similar explanation as to why so many new pizza places have popped up.

Benny Cappella's came along in 2015 followed by Lotsa Stone Fired Pizza in 2016, and 2018 has brought two new pizza places, the Pizza Press and MidiCi Italian Kitchen. With seven other pizza places also open on Franklin Street, these new restaurants have created a great deal of competition for student business. Unless UNC students and residents in Carrboro and Chapel Hill begin experiencing an extra-heightened love for pizza, people should expect to see some of these restaurants close in the near future.
